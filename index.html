Background, How To's and Examples of Great Alt Text

Great image alt text is important because it helps people understand images, especially those who can’t see them. Alt text describes what the image shows, making it easier for everyone to access the content. It’s also beneficial for search engines, which helps improve a website’s visibility. Writing effective alt text is a skill that anyone can learn.>

When writing alt text, it's crucial to be clear and concise. Use simple language to describe the image so that anyone reading it can understand. Focus on the key elements, such as the main subject and any relevant details, while keeping it brief. Avoid filler words, as they don’t add value and may confuse readers.

Another excellent practice is to consider the context of the image. Think about why the image is there and what you want users to take away from it. If an image serves a specific purpose, like illustrating a point in an article, make sure that is captured in the alt text. This will help create a better experience for users who rely on screen readers.

Lastly, it’s helpful to practice and get feedback on your alt text.Look at examples on various websites and assess them critically. By seeking constructive criticism, you can learn how to improve your writing skills and create better alt text over time. Here are some ways you can improve your great image alt text writing skills!

  • Be Descriptive: Clearly describe the main elements of the image without being overly detailed.
  • Focus on Context: Write alt text that relates to the message or purpose of the image within the content.
  • Keep It Short: Aim for a concise description, generally between 5 to 15 words.
  • Review and Revise: Regularly evaluate your alt text and ask for feedback to refine your skills

Here are some examples of Great Image Alt Text

  • "A smiling woman holding a basket of fresh vegetables in a sunlit garden."
  • "A close-up of a chocolate cake with strawberries on top, displayed on a wooden table."
  • "A group of children playing soccer in a park during a sunny afternoon."
  • "A mountain landscape with a clear blue sky and a winding river below."
